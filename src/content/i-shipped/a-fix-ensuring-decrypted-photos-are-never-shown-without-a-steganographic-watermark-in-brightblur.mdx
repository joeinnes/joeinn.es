Brightblur embeds an invisible steganographic watermark (a hidden per-viewer identifier) in each photo when it's displayed, so if a photo is leaked it can be traced back to who viewed it. I found a bug where decrypted photo data was being published to the display URL before the watermark was applied. I fixed the pipeline so the watermark is always embedded before the photo is ever shown.
